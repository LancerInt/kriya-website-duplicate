Mode of Action

Mycorrhizal fungi colonize feeder roots and develop an external hyphal network that e...

Mycorrhizal fungi colonize feeder roots and develop an external hyphal network that extends beyond the depletion zone surrounding roots. This symbiosis stimulates root growth, enhances uptake of phosphorus and other immobile nutrients, improves water extraction from soil, and increases tolerance to abiotic stress during establishment and active growth.
