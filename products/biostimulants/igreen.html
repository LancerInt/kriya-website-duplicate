Mode of Action

The microbial consortium colonizes the rhizosphere and root surface, fixes atmospheri...

The microbial consortium colonizes the rhizosphere and root surface, fixes atmospheric nitrogen through associative N-fixing members, solubilizes bound phosphorus and mobilizes native potassium pools, and drives microbial nutrient cycling in the soil. This combined activity improves nutrient availability, root uptake efficiency, and overall crop nutrition.

Primary Benefits

Built for nutrient mobilization and soil health

  • nitrogen availability
  • phosphorus availability
  • potassium mobilization
  • fertilizer-use efficiency
  • rhizosphere activity
Pests & Diseases Addressed

Biological suppression of key soil threats

  • Damps-off pathogens (Pythium, Rhizoctonia, Fusarium) in nursery and field settings.
  • Root lesion and root knot nematodes via rhizosphere colonization.
  • Foliar opportunists including mildews and blights when applied as foliar drench.
  • Secondary pests by strengthening plant resilience and metabolite production.

Program Fit

Use sites, compatibility, and storage

map Use Sites

  • Soil drench and drip irrigation programs for vegetables and specialty crops.
  • Seed coating and transplant dips for cereals, oilseeds, and nurseries.
  • Fertigation through greenhouse, hydroponic, and shade-net systems.
  • Orchards and plantation crops requiring sustained nutrient support.

science Compatibility

  • Do not mix with antimicrobial dressers, strong fungicides, bactericides, or highly saline concentrates.

inventory Shelf Life & Storage

  • Consortium strength: 1 × 10⁹ CFU/g.
  • Typical shelf life: 12 months in the unopened original pack under cool, dry, shaded storage.
  • Store sealed in the original pack away from direct sunlight, heat, and moisture.
