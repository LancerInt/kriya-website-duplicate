Mode of Action

Pseudomonas fluorescens colonizes the rhizosphere and root surface, competes for iron thro...

Pseudomonas fluorescens colonizes the rhizosphere and root surface, competes for iron through siderophore production, and suppresses pathogens via antibiotics, lytic enzymes, and other secondary metabolites. This microbial occupation also primes plant defense responses and reduces the activity of root-zone pathogens.

Use sites, compatibility, and storage

map Use Sites

  • Berry tunnels and net-houses

science Compatibility

  • Avoid mixing with fungicides, bactericides, strong oxidizers, alkaline materials, or concentrated salts.

inventory Shelf Life & Storage

  • Viable cell count: 1 × 10⁹ CFU/g.
  • Typical shelf life: 12 months in the unopened pack under cool, dry storage below 25°C and away from sunlight.
  • Store sealed in a cool, dry, shaded place and avoid heat, sunlight, and moisture that reduce viability.
