Mode of Action

Neem oil coats insect and egg surfaces, disrupting spiracular respiration and cuticular in...

Neem oil coats insect and egg surfaces, disrupting spiracular respiration and cuticular integrity, while associated neem limonoids act as feeding deterrents and mild insect growth regulators. Exposure interferes with endocrine control of molting, lowers feeding intensity, and suppresses oviposition and egg hatch in treated populations.

Crop Stage Dose per Acre Water Volume Application Method
Egg, nymph, and soft-bodied pest stage 500–750 mL/acre 150–200 L Foliar spray with complete surface wetting

Application Timing & Frequency

Application Timing Frequency Recommendation
At first detection of eggs, nymphs, or small larvae Repeat every 5-7 days Apply during morning or late afternoon to improve spreading and reduce heat stress on the crop.
During persistent soft-bodied pest pressure 2-4 sprays as needed Reapply after heavy wash-off and maintain coverage on the lower leaf surface.
Agronomic Challenges

Problem & Solution

Problem

Soft-bodied pests and eggs are escaping contact from conventional sprays.

check_circle

Solution

Use neem oil with high coverage to coat colonies, suppress hatch, and reduce feeding.

warning

Problem

check_circle

Solution

Repeat short-interval applications to suppress successive egg and nymphal flushes.
