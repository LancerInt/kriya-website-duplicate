Mode of Action

Metarhizium anisopliae conidia colonize the insect cuticle, germinate, and penetrate by en...

Metarhizium anisopliae conidia colonize the insect cuticle, germinate, and penetrate by enzymatic degradation involving proteases, chitinases, and lipases. The fungus proliferates inside the host, produces toxic secondary metabolites, and suppresses insect survival through systemic fungal colonization and mycosis.

Rexora application guidance

Rexora – Metarhizium anisopliae 1 × 10⁹ CFU/g WP

Crop Stage Dose per Acre Water Volume Application Method
Early foliar infestation or soil pest detection 500–750 g/acre 150–200 L Foliar spray or root-zone drench depending on target pest ecology
Established soil or canopy pest pressure 1.0–1.5 kg/acre 200–250 L Soil drench, drip application, or heavy-volume foliar spray

Application Timing & Frequency

Application Timing Frequency Recommendation
At initial pest establishment when humidity is favorable Repeat every 10-14 days Use the higher dose for persistent soil insect pressure or dense crop canopies.
During continuous emergence of target stages 2-3 applications Apply to moist soil or humid canopies to improve conidial infection efficiency.

Problem & Solution

Problem

Persistent field populations of soil or canopy insects are difficult to suppress biologically.

check_circle

Solution

Use Metarhizium where moisture allows conidial establishment and repeated infection cycles.

warning

Problem

Soil insect pressure is reducing stand and root vigor.

check_circle

Solution

Direct the product to the root zone or soil surface to target insect stages near the rhizosphere.

Metarhizium WP positioned for biological management of persistent insect populations, particularly where soil-associated stages are important.

Program Fit

Use sites, compatibility, and storage

map Use Sites

  • Soil drench in plantations and orchards
  • Seedling trays and nursery beds
  • Field crop furrow and band applications
  • Golf greens and turf
  • Greenhouse vegetables and ornamentals

science Compatibility

  • Avoid mixing with fungicides, bactericides, strong oxidizers, alkaline materials, or concentrated salts.

inventory Shelf Life & Storage

  • Viable conidial load: 1 × 10⁹ CFU/g.
  • Typical shelf life: 12 months in the unopened pack under cool, dry storage below 25°C and away from sunlight.
  • Store sealed in a cool, dry, shaded place and avoid heat, sunlight, and moisture that reduce viability.
