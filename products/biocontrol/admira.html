Mode of Action

Thyme oil constituents such as thymol and related phenolics disrupt cell membranes and alt...

Thyme oil constituents such as thymol and related phenolics disrupt cell membranes and alter neurophysiological signaling in exposed insects. The treatment acts as a feeding deterrent, interferes with immature development and oviposition, and suppresses pest establishment on treated foliage.

Application Timing & Frequency

Application Timing Frequency Recommendation
At first colonization of foliage or flowers Repeat every 5-7 days Spray during cooler periods to reduce loss of volatile actives and improve crop safety.
During sustained low to moderate pest pressure 2-4 sprays as required Maintain close spray intervals because persistence is shorter than conventional residual insecticides.
