Mode of Action

Azadirachtin functions primarily as a feeding deterrent and insect growth regulator

Azadirachtin functions primarily as a feeding deterrent and insect growth regulator. It disrupts ecdysone- and juvenile-hormone-mediated endocrine signaling, interferes with molting and metamorphosis, suppresses larval feeding, and reduces fecundity, oviposition, and egg viability in exposed pest populations.

Application Timing & Frequency

Application Timing Frequency Recommendation
At first appearance of nymphs or early larval stages Repeat every 7-10 days Use the lower dose as a preventive treatment and the upper dose under rising pest pressure.
During active flush or continuous pest emergence 2-3 sequential sprays Maintain full coverage of tender foliage and the lower leaf surface for better suppression.
Agronomic Challenges

Problem & Solution

warning

Problem

Sucking pests are causing curling, chlorosis, and honeydew deposition.

check_circle

Solution

Apply early to deter feeding and suppress immature development before populations peak.

warning

Problem

Repeated infestations are occurring after short-residual contact sprays.

check_circle

Solution

Use sequential applications to interrupt molting and reduce oviposition across overlapping generations.

Program Fit

Use sites, tank mix flexibility, and storage guidance

map Use Sites

  • Open-field vegetables, pulses, cereals, and cotton

  • Compatible with most neutral-pH spray inputs; avoid strongly alkaline mixes and confirm with a jar test.

inventory Shelf Life & Storage

  • Standardized to 3% azadirachtin.
  • Typical shelf life: 24 months in the unopened original container under cool, dry, shaded storage.
  • Store sealed in the original pack away from heat, direct sunlight, and moisture.
