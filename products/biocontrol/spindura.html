Mode of Action

Spinosad acts primarily on insect nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and secondarily on GAB...

Spinosad acts primarily on insect nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and secondarily on GABA-gated chloride channels, causing neuromuscular hyperexcitation, paralysis, and death. Treated larvae rapidly cease feeding, population growth is curtailed, and surviving adults often show reduced oviposition and lower egg viability.
