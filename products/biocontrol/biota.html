Mode of Action

Trichoderma colonizes the rhizosphere and root surface, competes aggressively for space an...

Trichoderma colonizes the rhizosphere and root surface, competes aggressively for space and nutrients, and parasitizes pathogenic fungi through enzymatic degradation involving chitinases, glucanases, and proteases. It also produces antifungal secondary metabolites and can trigger host defense priming, thereby suppressing soil-borne pathogens and improving root health.

Diseases Controlled

Target Spectrum

  • Damping-off pathogens (Pythium, Rhizoctonia)
  • Fusarium wilt and collar rot complexes
  • Sclerotium and Sclerotinia rots
  • Phytophthora blights and root rots
  • Nematode suppression through antagonism
  • Secondary suppression of foliar blights via ISR

Program Fit

Use sites, compatibility, and storage

map Use Sites

  • Nursery trays and propagation houses
  • Field transplanting and drip irrigation
  • Greenhouse vegetables and ornamentals
  • Orchards and perennial plantations
  • Hydroponic systems (low rate)

science Compatibility

  • Avoid mixing with fungicides, bactericides, strong oxidizers, alkaline materials, or concentrated salts.

inventory Shelf Life & Storage

  • Viable propagule load: 1 × 10⁸ CFU/g.
  • Typical shelf life: 12 months in the unopened pack under cool, dry storage below 25°C and away from sunlight.
  • Store sealed in a cool, dry, shaded place and avoid heat, sunlight, and moisture that reduce viability.
