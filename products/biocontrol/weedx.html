Mode of Action

Contact-active fatty acids solubilize cuticular lipids and disrupt cell membranes, causing...

Contact-active fatty acids solubilize cuticular lipids and disrupt cell membranes, causing leakage, desiccation, and rapid loss of mobility in exposed pests. Feeding stops soon after contact, egg viability is reduced on treated surfaces, and repeated coverage suppresses population establishment and reproduction.

Crop Stage Dose per Acre Water Volume Application Method
Soft-bodied pest or egg stage 750–1000 mL/acre 150–200 L Foliar contact spray with complete wetting

Application Timing & Frequency

Application Timing Frequency Recommendation
At first appearance of exposed colonies Repeat every 4-7 days as required Reapply promptly because residual persistence is short and new contact is needed for control.
During rapid colony expansion 2-4 applications Target exposed insects directly and avoid use under high heat on sensitive foliage.
